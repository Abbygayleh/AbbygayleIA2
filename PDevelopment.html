Twelve Pillars

Chapter 4

Summary:

In Chapter 4, Michael shows worry about his professional life, feeling as though he isn't reaching his full potential. Charlie teaches the concept of goal-setting and stresses the significance of having specific objectives in life. Charlie then suggests that Michael put his goals in writing because doing so helps one become conscious of their goals and motivates them to be achieved.


Application to my life:

I would start by identifying my goals by listing both short and long-term objectives for my profession, education, personal development, and the list goes on. I would then do as Charlie advised which is writing down my goals. I would be as clear as I can about what I hope to accomplish and when. Lastly, I would keep my objectives in sight, possibly by displaying them somewhere I'll see them frequently in order to stay motivated and to remind myself of the reasons I originally established these goals.



Chapter 5

Summary:

In Chapter 5, time management is the topic of a conversation between Sue, Charlie, and Michael. Charlie explains the value of setting objectives and making the most of every day to Michael, who is feeling burdened with his career and life. Charlie quotes Mr. Davis as saying that people must make a decision between the "pain of discipline" and the "pain of regret," and that they must master time by creating their own life plans as opposed to allowing time to control them. Charlie is thanked by Michael, who also makes a commitment to return to him shortly.


Application to my life:

I would develop discipline by accepting the "pain of discipline" by following my timetable and abstaining from procrastinating. I would recognize that over time, little, persistent efforts add up. Reflect on my regrets and use the "pain of regret" as motivation to take focused action. I would think about how I would feel if I didn't pursue your objectives. In addition to this, I would make a daily timetable or to-do list that supports my life strategy and also set up defined time periods for jobs and activities, making sure they advance my goals. The most important part of this all is for me to be willing to apply these principles, just as how Charlie portrayed this trait throughout chapters 4-7.



Chapter 6

Summary:

In Chapter 6, Michael visited Charlie at Twelve Pillars sharing wonderful news. The news stated that his wife and he were getting better, and he had made a list of 100 life goals. The third Pillar of Success is to surround oneself with the finest people, not simply the best things, Charlie informed Michael.

Application to my life:

My life may be positively affected by surrounding oneself with uplifting, encouraging, and growth-minded people. This is so because my goals and decisions are greatly influenced by people in my social circle. With that being said, I would ensure that I surround myself with positive and supportive people. By doing this I would also consider whether my pals are advancing my development, learning, and pursuit of my objectives or impeding it.
